Accomodations

Where to rest your head

Camping on the Playa

For the most immersive experience, camping directly on the Alvord Desert playa is permitted and free. Wake up to sunrise over the desert floor and fall asleep under a canopy of stars. This is our top recommendation for adventurous guests.

  • No facilities—bring everything you need
  • Leave no trace principles apply
  • Best for self-sufficient campers with proper gear

Alvord Hot Springs

Located right at the edge of the playa, Alvord Hot Springs offers a few rustic cabins and camping spots with access to natural hot spring pools. Book well in advance—it's a popular spot.

Fields Station

The tiny town of Fields (population: ~6) has a small store, cafe famous for its milkshakes, and basic lodging. It's the closest thing to civilization near the desert.

Burns, Oregon

For those preferring traditional lodging, the town of Burns is about 1.5 hours from the playa and offers several motels and hotels:

  • Silver Spur Motel — Clean, affordable, locally owned
  • Best Western Plus — Reliable chain option with standard amenities
  • Historic Central Hotel — Charming historic property in downtown Burns

Frenchglen

About an hour from the playa, the historic Frenchglen Hotel is a charming option. This small, rustic hotel offers a glimpse into Oregon's ranching heritage. Reservations are essential.
