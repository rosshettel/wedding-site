The Alvord Desert

A landscape like no other

About This Magical Place

The Alvord Desert is a 12-mile long alkaline playa in Harney County, Oregon, resting in the rain shadow of Steens Mountain. When dry, the lakebed becomes a vast, cracked expanse of white that stretches to the horizon—a surreal and otherworldly landscape.

We chose this location because it represents the vast, open possibilities of our future together. The stark beauty and infinite horizon feel like the perfect backdrop for beginning our married life.

What to Expect

The playa sits at about 4,000 feet elevation. Temperatures can vary dramatically—warm days and cool nights are common. The landscape is flat, dry, and incredibly photogenic.

  • Terrain: Hard-packed alkaline clay when dry, impassable mud when wet
  • Elevation: Approximately 4,025 feet
  • Best feature: Endless horizon with Steens Mountain as a dramatic backdrop
  • Night sky: Some of the darkest skies in the continental US

What to Bring

  • Sunscreen and sunglasses—there's no shade on the playa
  • Layers for temperature changes
  • Comfortable shoes (the surface can be rough)
  • Plenty of water
  • A camera—you'll want to capture this
  • A sense of adventure

Nearby Hot Springs

The area is home to several natural hot springs, including Alvord Hot Springs just off the playa. After the celebration, a soak under the stars is highly recommended.
